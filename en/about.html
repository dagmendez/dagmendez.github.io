About Me

Add your image as images/profile.jpg

Hey! Have you ever wondered how I got here? Like many of my millennial peers, I was the family's "official tech guy" – from setting up computers to fixing any IT crisis. While my youth was defined by hours of gaming on Windows, I also dove into hardware (building PCs from scratch) and Linux experimentation.

But here's the twist: I studied Economics and then did an MBA in International Trade. It was during my second year as an analyst that I faced a challenge: processing monster Excel spreadsheets on an office computer that, let's say, wasn't up to the task.

Thanks to my years of tinkering with the terminal, I wasn't afraid of the black screen. So I dove into data analytics: first with R and Python, then moved to data engineering with Spark and Scala. One thing led to another, and suddenly I found myself working in the systems where data is actually generated, transitioning from data analyst to backend engineer in distributed systems.

And as if that weren't enough, the cloud became the king of enterprise environments. So my tech stack evolved: from HDFS, Spark and MongoDB to S3, Databricks, DynamoDB and Kinesis (or their equivalents in Azure/GCP). Because technology never stops, and neither do I.
